Appeals, Reconsiderations, Judicial Reviews & Inadmissibility

About This Service

If your application has been refused or you're facing complex immigration issues like inadmissibility, we provide strategic guidance and representation for appeals, reconsiderations, judicial reviews, and challenging cases.

Services We Provide

  • Refusal Analysis & Strategy: Understanding reasons and developing response plan
  • Reconsideration Requests: Asking IRCC to reconsider based on new evidence or error
  • Appeals (IAD): Immigration Appeal Division hearings
  • Judicial Reviews (Federal Court): Challenging decisions in Federal Court
  • Procedural Fairness Responses: Responding to concerns before decision
  • Inadmissibility Cases: Criminal, medical, misrepresentation, or security inadmissibility
  • Removal Orders: Challenging or staying removal orders
  • Restoration of Status: Restoring status after expiry
  • Pre-Removal Risk Assessment (PRRA): For removal order subjects

Common Refusal Reasons

Temporary Residence Applications

  • Purpose of Visit: Officer not satisfied about true intentions
  • Ties to Home Country: Insufficient evidence of ties
  • Financial Capacity: Inadequate funds or insufficient proof
  • Travel History: Lack of travel history or overstays elsewhere
  • Dual Intent: Appearing to have immigration intent for temporary visa

Study Permits

  • Study Plan: Unconvincing educational progression or goals
  • Financial Capacity: Insufficient funds for tuition and living expenses
  • Ties to Home Country: Concerns about returning after studies
  • Academic Background: Gaps or questionable educational history

Work Permits

  • LMIA Issues: Problems with Labour Market Impact Assessment
  • Qualifications: Not meeting job requirements
  • Employer Compliance: Concerns about employer
  • Dual Intent: Immigration concerns for temporary work permit

Permanent Residence

  • Misrepresentation: Providing false information or documents
  • Ineligibility: Not meeting program requirements
  • Inadmissibility: Criminal, medical, security, or financial reasons
  • Documentation: Missing or insufficient supporting documents
  • Relationship Genuineness: For spousal sponsorship

Response Options After Refusal

1. Reconsideration Request

  • Ask IRCC to reconsider the decision
  • Based on new evidence not previously available
  • Based on factual error or misinterpretation of evidence
  • No formal right to reconsideration - relies on IRCC discretion
  • Timeline: No set timeline; usually submit within 30-60 days of refusal

2. Reapply

  • Submit a new application addressing refusal reasons
  • Strengthen weak areas identified in refusal letter
  • Provide additional evidence
  • Must pay new application fees
  • Best option if refusal based on insufficient evidence

3. Appeal to Immigration Appeal Division (IAD)

Right of appeal available for:

  • Refused family sponsorship applications (spousal, dependent, parent/grandparent)
  • Residency obligation determinations for PRs
  • Removal orders (for PRs and certain foreign nationals)
  • Sponsor not meeting sponsorship obligations

Timeline: 30 days to file notice of appeal

Process: Full hearing before IAD member; can present evidence and witnesses

4. Judicial Review (Federal Court)

  • For decisions with no right of appeal
  • Challenges whether decision was reasonable
  • Based on errors of law, fact, or process
  • Does not re-hear case; reviews whether decision-making was proper
  • Timeline: 15 days for removal order; 60 days for other decisions
  • Must first request leave (permission) to commence judicial review
  • If successful, case sent back to IRCC for redetermination

Inadmissibility Issues

Types of Inadmissibility

  • Criminal: Convictions or charges for offenses
  • Medical: Health conditions posing danger or excessive demand
  • Financial: Inability to support oneself
  • Security: Espionage, subversion, terrorism
  • Misrepresentation: Providing false information
  • Non-Compliance: Failing to comply with IRPA
  • Family: Family member who is inadmissible

Overcoming Inadmissibility

  • Criminal Rehabilitation: For criminal inadmissibility (after 5+ years)
  • Temporary Resident Permit (TRP): Temporary entry despite inadmissibility
  • Record Suspension (Pardon): Canadian criminal records
  • Medical Inadmissibility: Mitigation plans or individual assessments
  • H&C Considerations: For certain inadmissibility types

Procedural Fairness Letters

Before refusing an application, IRCC may send a procedural fairness letter:

  • Outlines concerns about your application
  • Provides opportunity to respond before decision
  • Typically short deadline (7-30 days)
  • Critical to respond comprehensively and promptly
  • Failure to respond adequately often results in refusal

Misrepresentation

One of the most serious issues in immigration:

  • 5-year ban from Canada if found to have misrepresented
  • Can include innocent mistakes or omissions
  • Applies to material facts (facts that could influence decision)
  • Can affect future applications even if current one withdrawn
  • Critical to address allegations thoroughly

Removal Orders

Types

  • Departure Order: Must leave within 30 days; can return if leave properly
  • Exclusion Order: 1 or 2-year ban; need ARC to return
  • Deportation Order: Permanent ban; need ARC to return

Options

  • Appeal to IAD (if eligible)
  • Stay of removal pending appeal
  • Pre-Removal Risk Assessment (PRRA)
  • H&C application
  • Judicial review
  • Authorization to Return to Canada (ARC) - if already removed
