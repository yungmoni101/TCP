Start-Up Visa, Self-Employed, Provincial Business Programs

About This Service

Canada welcomes entrepreneurs, investors, and self-employed individuals who can contribute to the Canadian economy. We guide business immigration through federal and provincial programs.

Services We Provide

  • Start-Up Visa Program: For innovative entrepreneurs
  • Self-Employed Persons Program: For cultural and athletic professionals
  • Provincial Entrepreneur Programs: Business immigration through provinces
  • Owner-Operator LMIA: For business owners seeking work permits
  • Intra-Company Transfers: For executives and managers
  • Business Plan Development: Strategic business planning for immigration

Start-Up Visa Program

For entrepreneurs with innovative business ideas:

Requirements

  • Have a qualifying business - innovative, can create jobs, can compete globally
  • Get support from designated organization:
    • Venture capital fund: minimum $200,000 investment
    • Angel investor group: minimum $75,000 investment
    • Business incubator: acceptance into program
  • Language proficiency: CLB 5 in English or French
  • Sufficient settlement funds
  • Up to 5 people can apply as co-founders

Benefits

  • Permanent residence pathway
  • Can get work permit while PR application processes
  • No requirement to reside in specific province
  • PR not dependent on business success

Self-Employed Persons Program

For individuals with experience in cultural activities, athletics, or farm management:

Eligibility

  • Relevant experience:
    • 2 years of self-employment in cultural activities or athletics
    • 2 one-year periods at world-class level in cultural or athletic activities
    • 2 one-year periods in farm management
  • Intention and ability to be self-employed in Canada
  • Meet selection criteria (100 points system - need 35+ points)
  • Make significant contribution to cultural/athletic life or Canadian economy

Assessment Factors

  • Experience (max 35 points)
  • Education (max 25 points)
  • Age (max 10 points)
  • Language proficiency (max 24 points)
  • Adaptability (max 6 points)

Provincial Entrepreneur Programs

Many provinces offer entrepreneur streams:

Common Features

  • Work permit stage followed by nomination for PR
  • Minimum net worth requirements (varies $300,000-$600,000+)
  • Minimum investment in business ($100,000-$500,000+)
  • Business plan and feasibility study
  • Active management requirement
  • Job creation requirements

Popular Provincial Programs

  • BC Entrepreneur Immigration: Regional and base streams
  • Alberta Immigrant Nominee Program: Farm Stream, Graduate Entrepreneur Stream
  • Saskatchewan Entrepreneur Program: Expression of interest system
  • Manitoba Business Investor Stream: Entrepreneur pathway
  • Ontario Entrepreneur Stream: For established businesses

Owner-Operator LMIA

For business owners who want to work in their Canadian business:

  • Must own at least 50% of Canadian business
  • Company must be incorporated federally or provincially
  • Demonstrate business will create jobs for Canadians
  • Show economic benefit to Canada
  • Two-stage process: LMIA + Work Permit

Quebec Business Programs

Quebec has separate business immigration:

  • Entrepreneur Program: For business owners/operators
  • Self-Employed Worker Program: For professionals and farmers

Key Considerations

  • Due Diligence: IRCC and provinces conduct thorough checks on source of funds
  • Business Viability: Must demonstrate realistic and viable business plan
  • Settlement Funds: Must show ability to settle in Canada
  • Market Research: Understanding Canadian market crucial
  • Compliance: Must meet all conditions of work permit and investment
