Spousal Sponsorship, Family Classes & Bringing Loved Ones Together

Family reunification is a core part of Canada's immigration system. Canadian citizens and permanent residents may be eligible to sponsor certain family members to live with them in Canada permanently. Sponsorship applications are closely reviewed, and strong documentation is essential to demonstrate eligibility and genuine family relationships.

We guide families through the sponsorship process with care, accuracy, and attention to detail at every stage.

Family Sponsorship Services

We assist with a wide range of family-based immigration applications, including:

  • Spousal & Common-Law Partner Sponsorship
    Applications for spouses or partners residing in or outside Canada
  • Dependent Children Sponsorship
    Sponsorship of eligible biological or adopted children
  • Parent & Grandparent Sponsorship
    Support for sponsorship applications when the program is open
  • Super Visa Applications
    Long-term visitor options for parents and grandparents when sponsorship is not immediately available
  • Other Family Sponsorships
    Assistance in limited circumstances permitted under immigration law

Spousal & Partner Sponsorship

Sponsorship applications for spouses and partners require careful preparation and clear evidence of a genuine relationship. We assist clients in presenting their relationship accurately and consistently, whether the application is submitted from inside or outside Canada.

Parents & Grandparents

Sponsorship of parents and grandparents is subject to specific program availability and income requirements. Where permanent sponsorship is not available or while waiting for an opportunity to apply, we assess eligibility for alternative options such as the Super Visa, which allows extended stays in Canada.

How We Support You

Family sponsorship applications can be emotionally significant and procedurally complex. Our role is to help ensure your application is complete, credible, and well-organized. We assist by:

  • Assessing eligibility of both sponsor and sponsored family member
  • Advising on the most appropriate sponsorship category
  • Preparing and reviewing application forms and supporting documentation
  • Guiding the collection and presentation of relationship evidence
  • Identifying and addressing potential concerns before submission
  • Assisting with applications following refusals or requests for additional information

A Careful and Respectful Approach

Immigration officers closely examine family sponsorship applications to confirm eligibility and genuineness. We focus on helping families avoid common mistakes, misunderstandings, and documentation gaps that can lead to delays or refusals.
