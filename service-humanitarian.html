Exceptional Circumstances & H&C Grounds for Permanent Residence

About This Service

Humanitarian and Compassionate (H&C) applications provide an avenue for individuals who don't qualify through standard immigration programs but face exceptional circumstances. These discretionary applications require strong evidence and persuasive submissions.

Services We Provide

  • H&C Applications for Permanent Residence: From inside or outside Canada
  • Establishment in Canada: Based on ties and integration
  • Best Interests of Child: Considering impact on children
  • Country Conditions: Based on adverse conditions in home country
  • Family Violence or Hardship: Exceptional personal circumstances
  • H&C for PR Card Retention: Residency obligation exemptions
  • H&C for TRP (Temporary Resident Permit): Entry despite inadmissibility

What is an H&C Application?

H&C applications allow IRCC officers to grant permanent residence to people who would not otherwise qualify, based on humanitarian and compassionate considerations. These are discretionary - officers have significant authority to approve or refuse based on individual circumstances.

Key Principle

The applicant must demonstrate that requiring them to apply through normal channels from outside Canada would cause unusual, undeserved, or disproportionate hardship.

Common H&C Grounds

1. Establishment in Canada

  • Length of time in Canada
  • Employment history and economic integration
  • Community ties and involvement
  • Education and skill development in Canada
  • Property ownership and financial ties
  • Evidence of becoming integrated into Canadian society

2. Best Interests of a Child

  • Impact on children if applicant must leave Canada
  • Children's ties to Canada (school, friends, activities)
  • Children's health, education, and well-being
  • Separation from parent or caregiver
  • Best interests are important factor, but not determinative alone

3. Adverse Country Conditions

  • Civil war or armed conflict
  • Political instability or persecution
  • Lack of medical care for serious conditions
  • Economic collapse or extreme poverty
  • Gender-based violence or discrimination
  • Must show personalized risk, not just general country conditions

4. Family Violence or Abuse

  • Victims of domestic violence
  • Human trafficking survivors
  • Situations where relationship breakdown affects immigration status
  • Impact of returning to country of origin

5. Health Considerations

  • Serious medical conditions requiring treatment in Canada
  • Lack of available treatment in home country
  • Mental health considerations
  • Disability accommodations

Who Can Apply?

  • Foreign nationals in Canada without status
  • Temporary residents who don't qualify for PR through other programs
  • People outside Canada (with limitations)
  • Foreign nationals facing inadmissibility
  • People who don't meet conventional program requirements

Who Cannot Apply?

  • Refugee claimants whose claim has been referred to Immigration and Refugee Board
  • People subject to removal order (in some cases)
  • Sponsored family members whose sponsorship is still in process

Evidence Requirements

H&C applications require comprehensive evidence:

  • Establishment: Employment letters, tax documents, community involvement letters, property documents
  • Children: School records, medical records, psychological assessments, letters from teachers/counselors
  • Country Conditions: Country condition reports, expert opinions, news articles, personal affidavits
  • Hardship: Medical reports, psychological assessments, expert opinions
  • Letters of Support: From employers, community members, teachers, healthcare providers

H&C for PR Card Retention

Permanent residents who don't meet residency obligation can apply for H&C consideration:

  • Based on establishment in Canada before leaving
  • Best interests of child affected by loss of PR
  • Exceptional reasons for being outside Canada
  • Family violence or abuse situations

Processing & Considerations

Processing Time

  • Typically 18-24+ months
  • No fast-track option
  • Significant backlogs in some locations

Work & Study Permits

  • Can apply for work or study permit while H&C pending (fees apply)
  • Officers have discretion to issue permits
  • Not automatically granted

Success Rate

  • H&C applications are discretionary
  • Lower approval rates than standard programs
  • Require strong, well-documented cases
  • Quality of application and evidence critical

Application Strategy

Successful H&C applications require:

  • Comprehensive documentation of all H&C factors
  • Persuasive written submissions explaining hardship
  • Expert reports where appropriate (medical, psychological, country conditions)
  • Letters of support from credible sources
  • Addressing any negative factors honestly
  • Clear explanation of why normal process would cause unusual hardship

How We Help

  • Assess viability of H&C application based on your circumstances
  • Identify relevant H&C factors in your case
  • Develop comprehensive application strategy
  • Guide evidence collection and documentation
  • Prepare detailed legal submissions and hardship analysis
  • Coordinate with experts (doctors, psychologists, country experts)
  • Draft persuasive H&C grounds and arguments
  • Compile compelling letters of support
  • Apply for work/study permits during H&C processing
  • Respond to procedural fairness letters
