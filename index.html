Peter Poon

Founder & Chief Executive Officer

Peter Poon is a Canadian lawyer and licensed Regulated Canadian Immigration Consultant (RCIC R1040879), authorized to represent clients in Canadian immigration matters. He is also accredited to appear before the Immigration and Refugee Board of Canada (IRB – Level 3). With several years of professional immigration practice, Peter provides strategic, compliant, and client-focused immigration services to individuals and families worldwide.

Peter understands that immigration is more than a legal process, it is a life-changing decision that often involves uncertainty, pressure, and high expectations. He places strong emphasis on building trust, maintaining clear communication, and guiding clients through every stage of the immigration process with honesty, care, and attention to detail. His approach is practical, thorough, and firmly grounded in Canadian immigration law and policy.

As both a lawyer and RCIC, Peter brings a strong legal perspective to immigration applications, particularly in complex or high-risk cases. His practice includes temporary residence applications such as work permits, study permits, and visitor visas, as well as permanent residence pathways including Express Entry, Provincial Nominee Programs, and family sponsorship. He also has experience handling refused applications, procedural fairness letters, inadmissibility issues, humanitarian and compassionate considerations, authorization to return to Canada, citizenship matters, and cases involving appeal or judicial review considerations.

Having grown up in Canada, Peter has firsthand experience of the country's multicultural society and the opportunities it offers. This personal connection has shaped his commitment to helping clients pursue lawful, realistic, and sustainable pathways to build their future in Canada. He ensures clients are fully informed about timelines, requirements, and expectations before and throughout their application process.

Peter is known for his professionalism, approachability, and strong work ethic. Clients value his straightforward advice, attention to detail, and dedication to preparing well-documented, compliant applications rather than relying on shortcuts or unrealistic promises. He practices with a strong sense of professional responsibility and regulatory compliance.

Peter speaks fluent English and is continuously improving his Chinese & French. He looks forward to working with clients from around the world and supporting them in achieving their Canadian immigration goals with confidence and clarity.

FAQ

Ask me anything

Find answers to common questions about our services and Canadian immigration

01. What is Express Entry and am I eligible?

+

Express Entry is Canada's application management system for three federal economic immigration programs: Federal Skilled Worker, Federal Skilled Trades, and Canadian Experience Class. Eligibility depends on factors like age, education, work experience, and language ability. Book a consultation for a personalized assessment.

02. My visa was refused. Can you help?

+

Yes. We handle refusals and complex cases. We'll review your refusal letter, identify the reasons, and advise on the best course of action—whether reapplying with a stronger application, requesting reconsideration, or filing an appeal.

03. How do I sponsor my spouse or family member?

+

Canadian citizens and permanent residents can sponsor eligible family members. Requirements include proving your relationship, meeting income requirements (for parents/grandparents), and demonstrating you can support your sponsored relative. We guide you through the entire sponsorship process.

04. Do you work with single applicants, couples, and families? Do you work with clients outside Canada?

+

Yes. We work with single applicants, married or common-law couples, and families, both inside and outside Canada. Consultations and services can be provided remotely (voice or video conference) or in person, depending on your location and circumstances.

05. Who is responsible for my application?

+

Regardless of which consultant you initially speak with or book a consultation with, all applications are centrally overseen and managed by Peter Poon, CEO, who serves as the lead representative on all files. This ensures consistency, accountability, and compliance across every case.

06. What happens during a consultation?

+

During a consultation, we assess your background, discuss your immigration goals, review eligibility, explain available pathways, and outline potential risks, timelines, and next steps. No applications are submitted without a consultation.

07. Does buying a business or property give permanent residence in Canada?

+

Buying a business or property does not automatically grant permanent residence. However, buying a business or property in Canada can support and strengthen an immigration or permanent residence application when properly structured and aligned with an eligible program.

08. Can you help if my application was refused?

+

Yes. We assist with refused applications, including reviewing refusal reasons, advising on re-application strategies, and addressing procedural fairness or admissibility concerns where applicable.
